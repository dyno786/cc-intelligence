Probate leads — executors need to sell quickly
Deceased estates notices in The Gazette mean an executor is managing an estate and typically wants a quick, clean sale. Approach respectfully within 2-4 weeks of the notice appearing. Competition is very low — most investors don't know this source exists.
📰
Free
The Gazette — Deceased Estates
Notice type 1300/1301/1302. Same source as your insolvency panel but different notice codes. Executors publishing legal notice of estate administration — properties typically need selling within 12 months.
→ Leeds Deceased Estates → Yorkshire Deceased Estates → National Deceased Estates
⚖️
Free
Probate Registry — Grant Applications
When probate is granted, it becomes public record. Search by deceased person's name or postcode. Shows who the executors are and the value of the estate.
→ Search Probate Records (free)
✉️
Strategy
How to Approach Probate Vendors
Write to the executor (address from Land Registry title — £3). Reference the probate notice. Be respectful, state you are a cash buyer who can complete quickly. Use the Outreach Tool to generate an appropriate letter.
